Challenge 4: Foul Play

This is what it looks like to be on the receiving end of a foul ball at a baseball game.

Be careful out there! But who is responsible for this hit?

What is the name of the batter?

This Bellingcat challenge shows a video clip of a batter hitting a ball in a baseball game. You can see the video here.

Uniform search

One of the best ways to identify a player in a game is to analyze their uniform.

I can see the names "Bichette" and part of what appears to be "Guerrero Jr.", both of whom play for the Toronto Blue Jays.

From a quick Google search, the powder blue uniform also clearly belongs to the Toronto Blue Jays.

But the Blue Jays aren't the batting team, they're the fielding team. The batting team wears black and white.

Searching for away team baseball uniforms comes up with too many results that are black and white, to narrow it down.

Stadium details

The stadium has the word "Rogers" on it, as well as the "Corona Rooftop Patio", both of which seem to relate to the Rogers Centre.

There is also a sign talking about the "official hot dogs of the Toronto Blue Jays".

It seems clear that this is a Toronto Blue Jays home game.

There are far too many games played here for this to narrow it down.

Stadium signs

On the top right, the ticker is displaying number 31 and a name which looks like it starts with D.

This ticker is displaying the name of the batter, but it's quite hard to read.

Baseball websites

When I search for baseballers who wear the number 31 in the year 2022, 2023 or 2024, a few players wear the number 31 and start with D.

The name "DeLoach" looks like it could fit.

When I Google 31 Deloach, I find that he plays for the Chicago White Sox.

I look up Blue Jays vs White Sox at the Rogers Centre, and find an image with uniforms that appear to match the uniforms in the video.

In the Blue Jays vs White Sox game, the Blue Jays wore powder blue and the White Sox wore black and white.

The name of the batter who hit the foul ball is Zach DeLoach.
